These are the Few of My Favorites
The First Law Trilogy
Joe Abercrombie
Artwork by: Raymond Swanland & John McCoy

Abercrombie's characters are amazing and full of depth, and you're never quite sure what they're going to do because they're all so human, imperfect and prone to mistakes. There are no simple evildoers, no clear villains, no clear allies or rivals. Not only can we, as readers, ponder through them, but Abercrombie does a remarkably good job at making each character see the others through completely different lenses. So, while one character may be hated by all his old friends, he is seen as "the greatest man I know" by anyone he met later in his life, in another place and time.

The plot itself is immense, at the same time as the best epic fantasy, and surprisingly concentrated at the same time. The conflict unfolds on a variety of occasions at once. Whereas George R. R. Martin's novels do not have completely straightforward heroes and villains, the stakes are very clear. We're rooted for the good guys, even though the good guys aren't really good. The same cannot be said for the trilogy of the First Law. Indeed, we're going into books with certain preconceived ideas, and nearly all of them are damaged, not only skewed but completely shattered.

The Wilds
Sarah Streicher

A group of young women are on a chartered flight to Hawaii for a troubled girl's retreat named The Dawn of Eve when the plane goes down, killing the crew and stranding passengers on an island in the middle of nowhere. When each episode cuts back and forth between the time after the island and the time before it—usually cut back on discovering the story of each girl one per chapter—the "Lost" comparison is direct and clear, particularly as teenagers find that there is more about where they are and how they ended up there than it first seems. And some of the girls have taken secrets to the middle of nowhere. When we learn more and more about the stranded teenagers, their identities begin to separate themselves from each other. If there's a lead, it's potentially Leah who gets the first flashback episode and gets the suspicion that things aren't what they appear to be on the island, but the show is really balanced with the group.

Valorant
Riot Games
Artwork by: ExCharny

Valorant is a team-based tactical shooter and first-person shooter set to operate in the near future. Plays play as one of a series of agents, characters based on a variety of countries and cultures around the world. In main game mode, players are assigned either to the attacking or defensive side, each team having five players on it. Agents have unique abilities, each requiring charges, as well as a unique ultimate capability that involves charging from kills, deaths, or spike acts. Each player begins each round with a "classic" weapon and one or more "signature capability" charges. Other arms and capacity charges can be bought using an in-game economic structure that rewards money depending on the results of the previous round. The game has a variety of firearms from secondary weapons such as side arms and main weapons such as submachine guns, handguns, machine guns, assault rifles and sniper rifles. There are automatic and semi-automatic weapons with a shooting method that must be controlled by the player in order to be able to fire correctly.
